# Steps to Craft a 5-Modifier Jewel

## 1. Start with a Single Modifier

Choose a jewel and use Annul Orbs until only **one desirable modifier** remains.

Once you have a single good modifier, begin using **Chaos Orbs** until you hit a second modifier worth keeping.


## 2. Lock in Your Core Mods

Determine whether your good modifier is a **Prefix** or **Suffix**.

Use an **Omen** to add another modifier of the same type that works well with your first modifier.

> **Tip:** If you don't hit a useful modifier, Annul the jewel back down and try again.

## 3. Fill Out the Modifier Group

Use a **Greater Exalted Orb** to add two random modifiers.

Next, use a **Potent Liquid of Contempt** to convert those new modifiers into the same type (Prefix or Suffix) as your two main modifiers.

> If you miss the 50/50 outcome, you'll need to reset and try again.


## 4. Add a Desecrated Modifier

Now use **Desecrate** to try to obtain a modifier that complements your existing ones.

Use a **Preserved Cranium** during this process.

If you get an undesirable Desecrated modifier, you can use an **Omen of Light** together with an Annul Orb to remove the Desecration and try again.

## 5. Clean Up the Jewel

Use a **Sinistral Annulment** to remove one of the extra Prefixes or Suffixes that was added in Step 3.

Then use another **Greater Exalted Orb** to add a new modifier.

At this point, you can use **Chaos Orbs** to reroll the opposite side of the item. Since your desired Prefixes or Suffixes are already filled, they will remain unchanged while you continue crafting.


## 6. Finish the Fifth Modifier

Once you have your desired core modifiers, use a **Potent Liquid of Ferocity** to finish the jewel.


## 7. Perfect the Rolls

Use **Divine Orbs** to roll the numerical values until you're satisfied with the results.


## 8. Upgrade the Quality

Finally, use **Catalysts** to improve the quality of the modifier that benefits most from increased quality.

Congratulations! You now have a completed 5-modifier jewel.

The exact market changes every league, but these combinations are usually among the most desirable for endgame builds.

## Minion Builds 
- Minion Damage + Minion Attack Speed
- Minion Damage + Maximum Life

- Minion Damage + Minion Cast Speed

# High-Profit Strategy

Rather than trying to make the "perfect" jewel every time, focus on jewels that already have 2–3 premium modifiers that are commonly used together. Those sell much faster and require much less currency to craft. If you're chasing mirror-tier jewels, expect to spend a lot of currency resetting failed crafts.

# Mid-Tier Jewel Strategy

The goal of this strategy is to craft jewels that sell **quickly and
consistently**. Instead of chasing mirror-tier items every time, build
currency through reliable sales and reinvest those profits into high-end
crafts.

##  1. Find a Good Base

Start with a jewel that already has **one valuable modifier**.

If it doesn't, use your preferred crafting method until you hit a
modifier that players commonly search for.

> **Tip:** Always check the trade site before continuing. Some
> single-modifier jewels are already worth selling.


## 2. Build Around Your First Modifier 

Determine whether your modifier is a **Prefix** or a **Suffix**.

Continue crafting until you add a second modifier that naturally pairs
with the first.

Example: - Attack Speed + Critical Strike Multiplier - Spell Damage +
Cast Speed - Minion Damage + Minion Attack Speed


## 3. Add a Third Modifier

Use your preferred crafting method (Greater Exalted Orb, etc.) to add
another modifier.

Your goal is **three modifiers that work together**.

Don't worry about making a perfect jewel.


## 4. Know When to Stop

If the jewel has **three strong modifiers**, check the trade price.

Many three-modifier jewels sell quickly for solid profit.

Don't overinvest trying to create the perfect jewel.


## 5. Divine Only Valuable Jewels

Only use Divine Orbs if the jewel is already worth enough to justify the
cost.

Poor rolls are acceptable on lower-value jewels.


## 6. Price to Sell

Compare your jewel to similar listings.

If your goal is fast currency, price it slightly below comparable
jewels.

# Modifier Tier List

## S Tier (Always Craft Around)

-   Critical Strike Multiplier
-   Attack Speed
-   Cast Speed
-   Spell Damage
-   Projectile Damage
-   Minion Damage
-   Maximum Life
-   Energy Shield (ES builds)

## A Tier (Very Strong)

-   Dexterity
-   Intelligence
-   Critical Strike Chance

## B Tier (Good Fillers)

-   All Attributes
-   Accuracy
-   Stun Avoidance
-   Armour
-   Evasion
-   Fire Resistance
-   Cold Resistance
-   Lightning Resistance

## C Tier (Usually Reroll Unless They Fit a Niche)

-   Life Regeneration
-   Mana Regeneration
-   Flask-related modifiers
-   Low-value defensive stats
-   Utility modifiers with little trade demand

# The 2-2-2 Rule

Use this rule to decide when to continue crafting or sell.

-   **2 premium modifiers** → Keep crafting.
-   **2 premium + 1 decent modifier** → Check the market.
-   **2 premium + 2 decent modifiers** → Usually sell unless there is
    exceptional synergy.

# Long-Term Strategy

1.  Craft and sell many mid-tier jewels.
2.  Build a steady currency reserve.
3.  Invest profits into premium five-modifier crafts.
4.  Accept that not every craft needs to be perfect.
5.  Consistency beats gambling.
